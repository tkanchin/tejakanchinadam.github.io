Publication

Stress Detection using Wearables
My Master's thesis project. In this work, I have proposed a novel data-driven Machine Learning pipeline which is robust to data and label noise and can classify stress in real-world conditions based on user's phsyiological responses. The data was engineered from the wearable devices via mobile phones and labels were collected via periodical surveys. I have implemented various feature learning (Deep Learning) and feature extraction algorithms and applied semi-supervised (Label Propagation) learning with gradient tree boosting to classify stress. This work is the first in the literature to classify stress with minimal manual labeling and by the use of wearables, in real-world conditions.
Teja Kanchinadam, Jamie Payton

Wikipedia Vandalism Visualization
Many users contribute to Wikipedia. Some users contribute good stuff and some bad. Even though Wikipedia uses bots to differentiate the good vs bad user, it is still a challenging task. Data mining on the historical Wiki edits may help to get the bot powerful, a small pre-processing Visualization can help to detect the outliers and make the analysis robust. In this project, I have visualized the unstructured data of Wiki edits in multiple visualizations using d3.js and displayed the attributes of benign and vandal users to detect the outliers and common patterns.
